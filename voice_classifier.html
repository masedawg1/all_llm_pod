Speaker Classification Policy Evolution

V1 — Host-Only Classifier

WhisperX segmentation + Pyannote embeddings. ~9,000 host clips from 44 episodes. Guest audio excluded. Structural flaw: no guest hard-negatives → risk of confident mislabeling. An “unknown” bucket absorbed ambiguity, but this was incidental. V1 worked in isolation. It was fragile in principle.
V1 Observations
  • ~9,000 clips
  • 44 episodes
  • No explicit guest class
  • Unknown bucket mitigated contamination

V2 — Guest-Aware Scaling

Same pipeline. Added Clownfish speaker mapping and bulk guest extraction. Architecture unchanged. Data discipline changed.
V2 Dataset & Results
  • 61,682 clips
  • 198.46 hours
  • 4 hosts + 87 guests
  • Host vs Guest: ~99%
  • 5-class: ~96–97%

V3 — Embedding Upgrade (NeMo / TitaNet)

Replaced Pyannote embeddings with NeMo TitaNet. Solved over-segmentation (4 speakers → 8–9 clusters). Cleaner embedding manifold. V2 fixed the dataset. V3 quieted the feature space.
V3 Results
  • 4-host classifier: 99.38%
  • Guest classifier (93 classes): 97.67%
  • Reduced cross-host confusion

V4 — Duration Constraint & Production Reality

Training constrained to >5s segments. Embedding geometry improved. But production exposed confident cluster-level failures. High accuracy ≠ production reliability. Architectural pivot: Human-In-The-Loop mapping of NeMo speaker_ID → timestamped audio.
V4 Key Findings
  • >5s training constraint
  • Improved separation geometry
  • Confident production misclassifications
  • Shift to HITL verification
