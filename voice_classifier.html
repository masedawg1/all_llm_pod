Voice Classification Architecture

Speaker Classification Policy Evolution

This page documents the evolution of the multi-speaker classifier and the statistical constraints that shaped its training policy. Sub-5 second audio segments are unstable for embedding-based speaker classification. Version 2 introduced structural gating to enforce a stable embedding domain.

Version 1 — Host-Only Classifier

initial architecture
Version 1 transcripts were generated using a WhisperX + Pyannote pipeline that extracted ~9,000 host audio samples from 44 episodes via four binary classifiers. These samples were used to train a multi-speaker classifier capable of labeling the four hosts across transcripts at scale.

Guest audio was intentionally excluded. At the time, I saw no value in collecting it, and doing so accurately at scale would have been operationally tedious.

Weeks later — after viable persona models already existed — I recognized the structural flaw. Guest audio provides essential hard-negative examples. More critically, a host-only classifier without a guest class risks mislabeling guest speech as a host, contaminating downstream training data.

An “unknown” class did appear in the transcripts, capturing low-confidence non-host speech. While this mitigated immediate failure, it was not intentionally designed as a formal guest class. The system was structurally fragile — and that realization triggered the redesign that became V2.

Version 2 — Duration-Gated Classifier

statistical discipline
Version 2 introduced a structural constraint: the classifier was trained on ≥4s segments and applied only to ≥5s segments. Sub-5s segments were labeled “lizard” and inherited speaker identity via cluster propagation. This enforced operation inside the stable embedding regime.
