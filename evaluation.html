Diarization Model Evaluation

The diarization subsystem was revised following a concrete instability case observed in Episode E136 under WhisperX + Pyannote.

  • Rapid oscillatory flips under cross-talk

Structural Context

Across 80,333 canonical host segments, 37–48% fall under 4 seconds. Panel conversation is interruption-dense by structure. Short-window embedding variance therefore becomes a critical stability constraint.

Diagnostic Investigation

Embedding space visualization of the same episode demonstrated higher cluster dispersion and fragmentation under Pyannote embeddings. This suggested sensitivity to short-duration acoustic variance.

Alternative Architecture Evaluated

WhisperX + NeMo TitaNet-L (multiscale embeddings) was tested on identical audio inputs. Multiscale embedding aggregation reduced intra-speaker variance and improved cluster cohesion under rapid turn-taking conditions.

Cross-Modal Validation

Diarized assignments were correlated against sampled face-presence detection (segment start / midpoint / end). Persistent contradictions between visual presence and audio attribution were treated as instability indicators. Sampling preserved computational feasibility while maintaining signal fidelity.

Architectural Outcome

NeMo TitaNet-L multiscale embeddings demonstrated improved temporal stability, reduced UNKNOWN sandwich artifacts, and stronger cross-modal alignment relative to the Pyannote baseline. The diarization subsystem was therefore revised accordingly.
