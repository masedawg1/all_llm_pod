Diarization Model Evaluation

Comparative analysis of diarization architectures under high-interruption panel conditions.

Objective

Evaluate speaker diarization stability and transcription fidelity in multi-speaker, interruption-dense podcast environments (~37–48% of segments under 4 seconds).

Architectures Compared

  • Baseline: WhisperX + Pyannote embeddings
  • Proposed: WhisperX + NeMo TitaNet-L (multiscale embeddings)

Evaluation Dimensions

  • Embedding cluster cohesion (geometric separability)
  • Speaker fragmentation rate
  • Short-segment robustness (<4s)
  • Unknown “sandwich” artifact frequency
  • Cross-modal validation (face presence vs diarized speaker)

Embedding Geometry Analysis

NeMo TitaNet-L

Multiscale embeddings demonstrated tighter intra-speaker clustering and reduced fragmentation under rapid turn-taking conditions.

Pyannote

Higher dispersion and speaker-ID inflation observed in interruption-dense regions, suggesting greater sensitivity to short-window variance.

Cross-Modal Validation

Diarized speaker assignments were correlated against sampled face-presence detection (segment start / midpoint / end). Persistent contradictions between audio attribution and visual presence were treated as instability indicators.

Sampling-based validation preserved computational feasibility while maintaining signal integrity.

Conclusion

Under interruption-heavy, multi-host panel conditions, NeMo TitaNet-L multiscale embeddings demonstrated improved cluster cohesion, reduced speaker fragmentation, and stronger cross-modal alignment relative to the Pyannote baseline.

Final architecture selection was based on embedding stability, attribution consistency, and downstream dataset integrity requirements.
