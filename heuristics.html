The segment ends mid-sentence — the speaker almost certainly kept talking and the boundary is arbitrary. 🚨 ellipsis + lowercase A: "The reason it works is..." Both signals fire together — the two fragments almost certainly belong to the same speaker. 🚨 ellipsis + speaker flip A: "So what I'm saying is..." An unfinished sentence immediately followed by a new speaker — either the first speaker's tail was clipped or the boundary is misplaced. 🎤 backchannel bleed A: "That's the entire thesis of the argument." B (different speaker): "Yeah." Single-word backchannels are too short to build a clean embedding — diarization routinely misassigns them to the wrong speaker. 💬 discourse marker injection A: "That's a really good point." B (different speaker): "So the way I think about it..." Discourse markers signal a speaker resuming — their presence at a flip often means a continuous utterance was split and misattributed. ⚠️ period before conjunction A: "That's the end of the first argument." B (different speaker): "But there's another way to look at this." Conjunctions grammatically connect to what preceded them — a conjunction opening a new speaker turn suggests a single sentence was cut in half.
