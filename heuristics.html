Heuristic Diarization Repair

Transcript Forensics and Repair

WhisperX establishes canonical segment and word-level timestamps; NeMo is constrained to operate within those boundaries to assign speaker identity. One failure mode: NeMo assigned the wrong speaker. It surfaces in two ways — either a segment boundary is misplaced, or the label on a correctly-bounded segment is wrong.

Both leave fingerprints in the transcript. Some are structural — patterns in how segments begin or end. Others are subtler relics: statements that appear grammatically complete but carry cues that the system was operating at the edge of its confidence.

Why it matters: The downstream model trains on this data. A misattributed segment means the wrong persona learns the wrong words — LoRA adapters trained on corrupted labels will interpolate between speakers, and the resulting voice won't sound like anyone. At 460 hours across 538 episodes, even a 2% error rate poisons thousands of training examples.

The solution: Human-in-the-Loop heuristic repair — flagging suspect segments using lexical patterns, then routing them for human review, where the transcript and audio-file timestamp are presented rather than re-processing the entire corpus. ↗ Episode 115 HITL review

8 backchannel bleed
▼
Short affirmative utterances in rapid exchange fall below the reliable embedding window and get misassigned to the acoustically dominant local speaker. The boundaries are correct, the words are right — the embeddings simply don't have enough audio to build a clean voiceprint.
